## Technical vs. Fundamental Assessment

**The Disconnect**: SOFI's stock is down 29% YTD while the company just posted its best quarter ever. This isn't a fundamental deterioration — it's a sentiment/macro-driven selloff:

1. **Broader risk-off rotation**: Investors fleeing high-growth, high-beta names for defensives amid Iran conflict and recession fears
2. **Valuation compression**: Stock ran to $32.73 on euphoria, now mean-reverting. At $27+ it was arguably overvalued; at $18.79 the pendulum may have swung too far the other way
3. **Dilution concerns**: Stock-based comp continues to be a drag
4. **No fundamental catalyst for the decline**: Q4 was a beat-and-raise, CEO buying, new partnerships announced

**Conclusion**: This is a PRICE problem, not a BUSINESS problem. The business is executing at or above expectations. The stock is repricing for macro risk and multiple compression. For put sellers, this is the ideal setup — you're getting paid elevated premiums to potentially acquire a fundamentally improving company at a discount.
