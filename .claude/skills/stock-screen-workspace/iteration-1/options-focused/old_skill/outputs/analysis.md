## Executive Summary

SoFi Technologies is a high-growth fintech/neobank that just posted its best quarter ever — $1.025B revenue (+40% YoY), $318M adjusted EBITDA (+60%), and added 1M members in a single quarter. The stock has been destroyed YTD (-29%) not because the business deteriorated, but because of a risk-off rotation out of high-growth names, dilution concerns from a $1.5B equity raise in Dec 2025, and Fed rate uncertainty. With the CEO buying $1M at $17.88, JPMorgan upgrading to Overweight at $31, and 2026 guidance calling for $0.60 adjusted EPS (54% growth), selling puts into this elevated IV environment is the optimal play — you get paid handsomely for a stock you'd want to own at lower prices anyway.

## Risk Summary for Put Sellers

**Max risk**: Assignment at strike minus premium received. At $16 strike with $0.60 premium, worst case is owning shares at $15.40 effective cost.

**Realistic risk**: A recession or credit event could push SOFI to $10-13, meaning ~$2-5/share paper loss on assignment. This is why position sizing at 3-5% matters.

**What kills the trade**: 
1. Sudden credit blowup in personal loans (watch charge-off rates)
2. Fed hiking rates (would crush NIM and loan demand)
3. Another dilutive equity raise
4. IV collapsing to <40% (makes the premium not worth the risk)

**What makes the trade sing**:
1. Stock stabilizes at $18-20, puts expire worthless, repeat monthly for 30-50% annualized
2. Gradual grind higher toward analyst targets ($25-31), puts never threatened
3. S&P 500 inclusion triggers a squeeze, making puts worthless within days
